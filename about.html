A hobby with a history

400AD
Bells introduced by Paulinus, Bishop of Nola in Campania, to Christian churches to call people to worship.
1170

The bells of Canterbury Cathedral allegedly tolled themselves when Thomas-a-Becket was murdered.

1536
Many church bells were removed during the dissolution and desecration of monastic abbeys and buildings.
1677
The Campanalogia is published, a book that introduced popular new methods of change ringing still popular today, such as Grandsire and Stedman.
1896
Miss Alice White of Basingstoke was the first woman to complete a full peal (c 3hrs of ringing).
1939
For 6 years during WWII all bells were silenced, to ring only to inform of an invasion by enemy troops. Ringing resumed in 1945.
